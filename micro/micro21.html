  • Cassini Ovals 7th July, 2021
    $b = 0.4a, \ldots, 2a$ when $a = 3$

    A Cassini oval is a set of points, such that for any point $P$ of the set, the product of the distances $ | PP_1 | $, $ | PP_2 | $ to two fixed points $P_1$ and $P_2$ is constant, say $b^2$, $b > 0$.

    If the foci of this curve are at $(a, 0)$ and $(−a, 0)$, then the equation of the curve is: $$ ((x-a)^2 + y^2)((x+a)^2 + y^2) = b^4 $$
    I think they look cool!
