  • My dear humans, I'm going E.F.E 10th October, 2021

    Embracing Finite Entertainment - E.F.E

    I have ditched most of my electronic devices I use in my daily life and went back to mechanical, cheap yet sturdy and reliable appliances. The recent FB, WhatsApp, etc outage had zero impact on my life. I didn't even know about it and only found out days later!

    The mobile phone was (obviously) the most difficult device to replace. I didn't really use my iPhone a lot, mostly for browsing the web, setting alarms, using the torch, listening to music, occasionally playing mobile games and for what a phone does, calls and texting.

    Web browsing on a small device is honestly a poor experience. Posture, urge to lookup stuff and just mindlessly browsing is 21st century horror. I didn't have to change anything for this, I just browse the web on my laptop now.

    Setting alarms, this is important, this is discipline. My super easy and effective solution was to get a desk alarm clock. The Casio TQ-140-1B. Cheap yet loud, reliable and it just works! I don't know why but it feels so much better looking at a mechanical watch.

    My house in pretty much inside a jungle with wild snakes, boars, mongooses, mosquitoes, wild dogs, wild cats, bats and hundred different insect species. A torch/flashlight is very much essential as centipedes, millipedes, etc are simply not fun in the dead of the night. The DL63 by Eveready is fantastic! Huge, brass made, heavy with a bright flood fill light. Pretty much a hand weapon, lol. Only flaw is that it uses 3 D-type batteries.

    Finally, the big question. Calls and SMS. I bought a Nokia 105 Single SIM. Fantastic device. Runs for a week, has decent call quality. Simple and effective. I use the *99# USSD method for payments (UPI).

    Pause for a minute and relax. Think about the excessive electronics you have in your daily life. Infinite entertainment, social media, news etc. I love the olden days.

    • A print newspaper ends after a few pages, online news does not.
    • A photo album ends after a few pages, instagram does not.
    • Talking front to front with people is more raw and finite but interaction with people on Facebook, Instant Messengers, etc are not.
    • An alarm clock has one use, a torch does just what's needed. A camera is just for taking photos. I don't need a device which does all of this as well as browse the internet and more.
    • Paper/Pen over iPads, e-ink displays etc. Paper ends, pens go dry. iPads/writings tablets have comparatively, infinite space. Souless.
    • A paperback book is physical and beautiful. After reading it you keep it in your bookshelf. You remember the memories when you touch it, flip it, years later. An ebook/Kindle reader isn't beautiful. Years later you can't touch or flip it for the feels. You might also loose all your books if your account gets banned.

    I fear the day where we all are forced to use electronics for anything as simple as going to the toilet! Printed material, simple gadgets, pen and paper, etc will slowly fade awat and might find homes in museums and antique stores.

    I hope we keep creating these devices with modern materials (easy on the planet) and changing times keeping the simplicity constant. An alarm clock in the 1900s, 2000s and 2100s should just ring when required.

    Electronics devices shouldn't cage you, lock you in. Life isn't worth living without freedom.

    Be free, embrace finite entertainment, go retro, live your life like it was supposed to - finite.

  • Cassini Ovals 7th July, 2021
    $b = 0.4a, \ldots, 2a$ when $a = 3$

    A Cassini oval is a set of points, such that for any point $P$ of the set, the product of the distances $ | PP_1 | $, $ | PP_2 | $ to two fixed points $P_1$ and $P_2$ is constant, say $b^2$, $b > 0$.

    If the foci of this curve are at $(a, 0)$ and $(−a, 0)$, then the equation of the curve is: $$ ((x-a)^2 + y^2)((x+a)^2 + y^2) = b^4 $$
    I think they look cool!
