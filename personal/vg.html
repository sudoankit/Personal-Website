Video Games.

I tend to enjoy video games that have a large degree of freedom and allow experimental gameplay or the few unique indies that break the norms.

Unfortunately as I grow older, I simply do not have time to play anymore.

Recommended.

  • Clash Royale, iOS, Supercell
    This is the best mobile game till date. Infinite possibilities, infinite replayability. Polished to perfection. Fun banter with mates near and far, silly experimentations. Simply fun. Truly world class design. Moderately aggresive monetization but you're not a kid or an addict, right? Right?
  • Battlefield 3, PC, DICE/EA
    The last good battefield. May the last bullet, the roar of the F/A-18F, the might of the M1A2 Abrams and my tear for this franchise help it rest in piece. Press F, ladies and gentlement. Gently, please press F.
  • Dark Souls, 2, 3, PC, From Software
    Praise the Sun! Praise the Swamps! Praise Pontiff Sulyvahn!
  • Assassin's Creed, 2, 3....Valhalla, PC, Ubisoft
    ...is a good franchise! The recent ones have gotten less assassin and more ass but still breathtaking large worlds of history that you can live, explore and just have fun! I really love the tours! Epitome of video game world building.
  • Far Cry 3, 4, 5 ...6, PC, Ubisoft
    Might get a lot of flack for this but hey, hey people! the combat feels good, large maps to clear, multiple ways to approach a mission. Sometimes everyone needs a bit of mindless fun.
  • Elder Scrolls IV & V: Oblivion and Skyrim, PC, Bethesda Softworks
    You see that [x] button on your broswer? You can absolutely click on that. Joking. Elder Scrolls are just damn fine games and age just like a damn fine wine.
  • Dishonored 1 & 2, PC, Arkane
    Arkane makes some of the most fun, experimental games ever and this series is such a joy to play! Fast combat, clever routes to try, multiple ways to approach, interesting world and story all weave out an amazing game in perfect symphony.
  • Into the Breach, PC, MacOS, Subset Games
    Even though this rouge-lite mecha-chess is quite RNG based, the information entropy and UI design is just sublime! It's pacific rim chess! What's not to like?
  • Fallout 3, New Vegas & 4, PC, Bethesda Softworks
    Always love me some fallout! Big, large post apocalyptic world filled with quests, loot, monsters, bugs and areas to explore! V.A.T.S and Nuka Cola automatically make this a must play. Avoid 76.

I dislike turn based combat and RNG heavy games, ain't got time fot that! Heavy lore dumps and cutscenes every 10 mins are also a huge turnoff. Fetch quest, tired open world design are ok with me if the gameplay is fun and solid. I do not, absolutely do not play anything live service or MMORPGs.
