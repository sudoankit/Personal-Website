Video Game Backlog üéÆ | 2020

Oh boy, I haven't really played many video games from 2012 but I really wanna try these. I think video games are magical and letting you experience a story which you can control is fascinating.

I too am interested in computer graphics (rendering, ray tracing, general GPU optimizations and illumination) so video games for me are quite interesting to dissect and appreciate the CG technology evolving so rapidly though these years.

Digital Foundry has some great videos if you're starting out.

My first game was Speed Busters: American Highways, a 1998 racing game from Ubisoft. It was way ahead of it's time. Brilliantly developed race tracks, amazing OST, weather conditions!, ~3D graphics and ran great. In case you still want to try it out you still get from Ubisoft or GOG.

My second one was Toy Story 2: Buzz Lightyear to the Rescue, a 1999-2000 by Traveller's Tales, Activision and Disney. This was an amazing game, really a tough one for a kid like me. Great levels, great graphics, hard and fun. My first Souls-borne. lol

Over the years I have played quite a few but some memorable ones are Recoil, DOOM 95, Serious Sam, Age of Empires II and Age of Mythology, GTA Vice City, San Andreas, Pokemon Emerald, etc

I have categorized them loosely using genres and publishers/devs, I really enjoy most of them except horror, rogue lites/card/deck building, visual novels, interactive drama and turn based combat maybe except Pokemon.

Analysis

So if I put all of these on Howlongtobeat and check how much time it will take me to complete it gives a scary number: Around ~3200 hours.

3200 HOURS, shit!

So that's ~133 days of 24 hour session, I can max do 6 hour sessions per day so that comes out to be around 532 days. I obviously won't be playing every day, mostly during vacations, after a stressful, busy and heavy week, etc.

I'm fairly confident I have around 4-5 years of content with me and it's expanding always!

I'm busy and though games are magical, pieces of art, blah, blah they are ultimately forms of entertainment.

A costly hobby.

I can't promise or dedicate my time to this this much. I hope to complete these games at points in my life but I can't really look back sometimes, I'm pretty sure by the time I complete these, another 100s of quality games would have come out! I'm not sure whether I will play all or whether I will completely leave gaming when life gets harder.

Only time will tell.

At least when I retire, I can read this backlog and wonder if playing ALL or some of them was worth it or not?

Till then, Ankit.
