Video Game Log
  • Grim Dawn, Maybe, dropped after 5-6 hours
    Fun action-rpg but still an action-rpg aka mindless, repetitive slaying of hordes for RNG loot.
    • Well optimized, runs smoothly even in very low end hardware.
    • Lot's of builds, customizations that allows you to experiment and create a character you love.
    • Good environments, many quests though all are basically kill, fetch or go rescue/talk to someone.
    • Fun for a while, get's very repetitive later on - this is pretty much the whole genre imo, I am not a huge fan of action rpgs because of the randomized loot.
    • To experience/enjoy it fully you need to buy all the expansions - significantly more expensive than the base game.
    • Satisfying gameplay, poor bosses.
  • Novalands, Maybe, dropped after 6-7 hours
    Forager copy but with a bit of a charm. Surprising amount of content.
    • Very much a forager copy.
    • Fast progression in the first half, grindy in the later
    • So much casual and chiller than hardcore games like a Factorio.
    • Simple crafting, only a few items, just 32 items in the research tree
    • Content is very well introduced and unlocked
    • Cheap enough for the content it offers, still I don't really think it's enjoyable content in the later half as you're just chasing to unlock everything and be done with it.
  • Dredge, Maybe, dropped after ~6 hours.
    A well crafted lovecrafting fishing indie that wears off after a few hours.
    • Gameplay: fish -> sell -> upgrade boat to fish better, explore, make is faster -> repeat
    • The story and atmosphere is what it makes it so much intriguing. There are lite horror elements, eldritch horrors of the sea lurking, mutated fish and cults.
    • Very beautiful! Striking art style! Etched in my memories!
    • Grindy. QTE Fishing.
    • ~7-8 hours, maybe 10 for everything
    • Quests are either fish something or fetch something.
  • Vampire Survivors, Recommended, ~40 hours = all achievements
    Bullet-hell, rogue-like shoot 'em up. Great old-style, charming art-style. Very addictive. Very cheap.
    • You pretty much play a stage again and again and level up your characters with random weapon and power spawns. Why? to unlock more stuff.
    • There's a nice implicit story that makes it fun to grind for achievements
    • The power scaling is really well balanced, dopamine rushes all the time when you have chest dropping given 5x rewards, your character massacring hordes.
    • Gameplay wise it's nothing revolutionary, nor is the story. But it's fun and that's what matters.
    • The surface hides deep secrets, interesting mechanics. It's so much more than a mindless RNG bullet hell session.
    • In Vampire Survivors you don't just hunt vampires, you hunt achievements. Secrets and unlocks are layered perfectly. Secrets unfurl like a Matryoshka doll subtly startling your intrigue.
    • New unlocks make the gameplay crazy - the good kind. That high DPS slaughtering makes the dopamine hit, the soundtrack kicks and before you know, you’re addicted.
  • TOEM, Maybe, ~6 hours = all achievements
    An intriguing albeit childish story, charismatic and memorable characters, soulful writing, whimsical dreams, grandmother’s love and a satisfying camera system fills this black&white game with infinite colors.
    • Very short, completed the main game in 3 hours (~75% achievements), 100% main game in 4hours, 100% everything (game+dlc) in ~5.5 hours.
    • Basto Island, DLC (free) is quite irritating with it’s confusing level design and the day-night mechanism
    • For the full price, this is not worth it, sale? sure.
    • Gameplay is again, taking photos and figuring out simple puzzle.
  • Fashion Police Squad (FPS), Not Recommended, dropped after 1 hour.
    Unfortunately you need specific guns to kill specific enemies. That's so stupid and breaks the flow.
    • Really wanted to play it fully, snazzy art fitting to its name.
    • Fun, unique concept. Happy Boomer Shooter.
    • Very basic combat skills.
    • Unfortunately you need specific guns to kill specific enemies. That's so stupid and breaks the flow.
    • If you don't use that specific gun, enemies don't get hurt! So bad.
    • Imagine playing DOOM but you need specific weapons to kill stuff.
    • Irritating enemies that just spam and crash onto you.
    • Combat arenas cluttered and visually messy
    • Platforming frustrating as well and just useless.
    • Weapons do not feel good to use at all. No soul. No kickback.
    • Incomparable to DUSK or ULTRAKILL. Those are leagues ahead. After playing those, you just can't like this...

    • Charming.
    • Very little rouge elements (which is good as I hate RNG stuff)
    • Amazing sound effects esp. the funny voices.
    • Great degree of polish.

    • Decent story albeit predictable story.

  • Salt and Sanctuary, Highly Recommended, 21 hours = main story + 90% side content
    2D Souls from 2 great souls!
    • Absolutely inspired by From Soft games, Bloodborne and Souls, the hunter cape is available!
    • Pretty much 2D Dark Souls
    • Great sense of fear and reward from exploration
    • Almost 100 enemies, ~15 areas with loot and secrets
    • A bit of a grind for materials which are required to upgrade weapons
    • Quite unbalanced late game, 4-5 weapons can pretty much get you through the game
    • Many attributes, huge skill tree and more to experiment and customize,
    • Ranged is useless
  • Far: Lone Sails, Recommended, ~3 hours = main story, 100%
    An amazing, beautiful, atmospheric and soulful journey into the unknown.
    • Complete 100%(?) in ~2.5 hours.
    • Astoundingly beautiful world, atmosphere. Excellent world building.
    • Simple puzzles, I appreciate the design choice to keep this simple as the main experience is the journey you take.
    • A few moments give a sense of awe and wonder, made me realize what makes this medium so special.
    • The feeling of travelling into the unknown, you are scared what lies ahead yet you want to push ahead.
    • Great soundtrack and sound design.
    • A bit bland gameplay that involves mostly micromanaging your vessel. Robs to fully experience the visuals.
    • Very short.
  • Vanquish, Not Recommended, Dropped after 4 hours
    Low budget, lite Halo wannabe.
    • Set in the future where, Russia attacks US using advanced robots, etc
    • You play as a DARPA super soldier with a cool suit
    • Fast and fluid combat bogged by mindless enemy design and AI
    • Rocket powered sliding is cool.
    • Extremely cheesy dialogue, story and characters
    • Terrible boss design
      • First boss is a big spider robot
      • Second boss is an human with a suit as well but he is so fast and simply spams
        • Unbalanced, three rockets take him out
      • Third is just the first boss x 2
    • Visuals are NOT great for a game that came out in 2010, AC Brotherhood, Mass Effect 2 and others look so much better, Fallout 3 (2009) looks better
    • Visual information overload, so much stuff in the background, horizon, front and back mix terribly.
    • Bland Art Design. Everything is grey.
    • Very short, can easily blast through the easy mode campaign in 4 hours or less. I finished 50% in just 2 hours in normal.
